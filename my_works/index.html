Gentrification and Racial Diversity in New York City from 1980 to 2010


Purpose


I conducted a study on gentrification and racial diversity in New York City to fulfill my undergraduate senior thesis. The question I had in mind was: Does gentrification promote or diminish racial diversity in New York City? And furthermore, does gentrification occur in areas of high diversity or low diversity or mixture?


Approach


First, in ArcMap, income and house value data were collected to identify gentrifying areas by census tract in 1980 to 1990, 1991 to 2000, and 2001 to 2010.

Second, racial demographic data were collected to calculate the entropy index to measure diversity rate.

Finally, bivariate cluster analyses (Bivariate Local Moran’s I) were conducted in GeoDa to explore how gentrification interacted with diversity changes in each decade from 1980 to 2010.


Final Product Overview


Results from the bivariate cluster analyses showed that gentrification does indeed diminishes diversity than promotes it in New York City. However, it wasn’t in every scenario which gentrification was found to induce a homogeneous demographic. Harlem and Hell's Kitchen in Manhattan, contrarily, proved gentrification can coexist with social mixing.
