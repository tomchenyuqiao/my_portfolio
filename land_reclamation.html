Land Reclamation in Hong Kong (1973 - 2017)


Purpose


Hong Kong, in the heart of Asia, leverages its easy accessibility commercially and geographically. However, it is also hindered by its uneven topography, and limited developable flat lands. Land reclamation is a common solution to this issue by converting water bodies into land.


This project aims to examine urbanization and land expansion of Hong Kong from 1973 to 2017, particularly in water to land changes, reclamation ground. (Digital Image Processing)


Approach


Landsat 5 and 8 images of Hong Kong were collected and combined in ENVI. Afterwards, the images were stacked to perform supervised image classification (Vector Machine Classification) in ArcMap to yield the changes in land use.


Final Product Overview


Final maps below illustrate land use changes:

  • Reclamation ground from 1973 to 1987 focused on expanding living space. (e.g. Sha Tin)
  • More areas dedicated to economic developments after 1987 (e.g. Hong Kong International Airport).
  • Simultaneous inland urbanization of vegetation land.
